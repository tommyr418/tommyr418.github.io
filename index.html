About Me

I graduated college with a B.S. in Biomedical sciences. Originally with the goal of going to medical school, I worked in the healthcare industry for 2 years as an assistant to a gastroenterologist. I learned a lot about the day-to-day of a physician. I loved the deductive reasoning that doctors had to employ to reach a diagnosis and propose a treatment. However, I also saw the negative impact of the healthcare industry as a whole on the life of physicians. I decided to take my love of problem solving on a different path.

My first experience with coding was in high school where I learned Java. I had not programmed in years so I was very rusty. I decided to jump back into programming with Ruby because of how elegant it was to read. I tried to refresh my understanding of computer programming as fast as possible so I attended App Academy, a 1000 plus hour intensive coding bootcamp with a selective acceptance rate, and was able to learn new technologies on order to create several interesting projects: FacePlace, Chance Quest, and Action Archive.

I hope to take what I have learned and begin a new career in a field that continues to challenge me. I am prepared to take on the inevitable bugs that arise, learn new innovative technologies, and work with the brightest people that continue to innovate the way we interact with our world. I know that only through hard work and determination can I reach my goals and continue to grow.
